Section 01
What Is Open Source AI?

Think of "open source AI" like a recipe that anyone can read, copy, modify, and use — as opposed to proprietary AI (like ChatGPT) where the recipe is secret. Open AI models release their weights (the trained brain of the model) so anyone can download and run them. This matters because it means privacy, no monthly fees, and the ability to customize the AI for any purpose.

The Key Milestones: From Niche to Frontier

2023 — The First Wave
Meta released Llama 2 for free, sparking the modern open-source LLM movement. Mistral released their tiny-but-powerful 7B model, showing smaller models could punch above their weight. Open source was interesting but clearly behind proprietary models.

2024 — The Ecosystem Matures
Llama 3 matched GPT-3.5 level quality. DeepSeek V2 and V3 stunned the world: Chinese open-source models rivaling GPT-4 at 1/10th the cost. Qwen 2.5 proved Alibaba was a serious player. Tools like Ollama made local deployment accessible to non-engineers.

2025 — The Reasoning Revolution
DeepSeek R1 (January 2025) triggered a $1 trillion stock market drop — a free, open-source model matching OpenAI's o1 reasoning model. Llama 4 Scout arrived with the world's largest context window (10 million tokens). Qwen 3 introduced "thinking mode" to open-source. Chinese lab downloads overtook US models worldwide.

"China Open Model Week" (Feb 2026): GLM-5, MiniMax M2.5, and Kimi K2.5 released within days — all MIT-licensed, all competitive with Claude Opus and GPT-5.
